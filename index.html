PAST

Founded in 1967 as ‘Sushi Shop’ by an entrepreneurial couple, this Mom & Pop business flourished as a local staple for years, but had reached a plateau. They have since passed the mantle down to their three sons, who carry on this family-run business for a new generation. Their tenure has reinvigorated the business, developing a franchise with several new locations and expanding the menu.

This left them with one last issue to be addressed, their weak digital footprint. Any franchise worth their salt requires a strong digital presence to back it up. The first way to tackle that is with a new website. This means designing a digital user experience that is in-sync with their new brand identity, target market and business strategy.

PROJECT PROCESS

1. PROBLEMS

  • Small Brand Reach

    While ‘Sushi Shop’ has an established brand, this is a base of locally driven traffic, built up over years of history in that location and community word-of-mouth. This support is not a guarantee following the expansion to a multi-location franchise, so a broader appeal is necessary to those unfamiliar with this brand.

  • Outdated Website

    The old website is a relic, last updated in 1998, that is not up to date with modern web standards. Lacking responsive design, riddled with broken links, dated UI, and usability issues abound, this website needs serious renovation.

  • Encumbured Ordering System

    During peak business hours, a frequently backed-up phone-line presents a deterrent to customers trying to place orders and a situation ripe for human error with swamped employees keeping track of them. An additional method of placing orders needs to be provided.

2. GOALS

  • New Brand Identity

    Update the restaurant’s brand appeal with a modern cosmopolitan identity, tapping into a larger customer base. Develop a larger digital footprint. The old generic name ‘Sushi Shop’ will swap out for ‘Sushi Fuze’, a better reflection of a contemporary restaurant brand that is host to a fusion of dish types.

  • Responsive Design/User Flow

    Design a site and app that smoothly scales across a myriad of devices and screen dimensions. Also, develop a streamlined sitemap that minimizes the number of steps required to accomplish a goal.

  • Order Efficiency and Management

    Provide an additional method for customers to request take-out/delivery by implementing an online ordering system. Prove this is the more efficient alternative by offering a checkout process that has user-friendly task navigation and does not tax the working memory.

Profiles and User Journey

Developing several character profiles as points of reference, I use these summaries to explore the potential needs of users. I then select one profile and conduct a user journey to explore their possible pleasure/pain-points, touch-points, and thoughts as they navigate through all stages of the website. This evaluation helps shine a light on potential usability issues I may have overlooked.

Rachel

Details: In a relationship, skydiving instructor, 28 years old, thrill seeker, lives in an unincorporated area.
Quote: “I want someplace that offers light meals for lunch, but can also provide formal dishes for dinner events.”
Notes: Rachel loves the Sushi Fuze branded prepackaged shrimp wraps from the local market, yet would like to have meals delivered to her apartment complex. Her building, hidden down a poorly marked side street, is easy to miss and she would like an easy way to track the car delivering her food. She would also like easy access to her favorite dishes when ordering online.

Health consciousness: High
Time constraints: Medium
Meal variety: High
Digital proficiency: Medium
Hobbies: Rock climbing, swimming
Spends most time: Small airport runway
Social networks: Pinterest, Facebook

Adam

Details: Married, brand consultant, 41 years old, quality conscious, lives in the suburbs.
Quote: “I want a dining environment where I can hold business casual meetings with clients.”
Notes: Adam is searching for a new contemporary restaurant that works as an informal gathering spot with his colleagues and clients. The laid back nature of meeting at the restaurant, as opposed to the boardroom, allows for less time constraints. Yet, Adam is concerned about accommodations for larger groups and the schedules of others accompanying him. Therefore, he likes to set reservations and place some orders in advance for early evening meetings, taking into account the dinner rush.

Health consciousness: Low

Mike

Details: Single, night shift and on-call surgeon, 32 years old, efficient, lives downtown.
Quote: “I want a quick meal I can pick up on my way to work.”
Notes: Picking up a to-go meal is convenient for Mike, since the restaurant is on his way to work. With erratic work hours, an efficient and reliable service are highly appealing traits make Mike a frequent customer. He has very conservative pallet, in that he sticks with only a handful of familiar meals, opting not to explore different dishes.

Wireframe and Responsive Design

Adhering to the mobile first approach, I wireframe the entire website and then adjust dimensions to cover the various device screen sizes.

Digital Assets

With a UX structure in place, I design UI elements that adhere to the brand identity. After importing these deliverables into a copy of the wireframe, they overlay the structure and receive additional size adjustments.

Interactive Prototype

Finally, I export the UI assets from the Sketch program into Marvel-App to create an interactive prototype. This mockup can then receive user testing for further input.
